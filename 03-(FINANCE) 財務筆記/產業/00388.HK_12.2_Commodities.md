# 00388.HK — Commodities（12.24%）

## 成本結構分析

Commodities trading generated HKD 2.91 billion in FY2025[1], representing a distinct business vertical focused on precious metals and commodity futures. This segment requires specialized infrastructure for commodity clearing, warehousing coordination, and precious metals settlement protocols. Cost structure includes fees for physical metals safeguarding, specialized compliance personnel for commodity regulations, and dedicated market surveillance systems. However, commodities trading leverages existing technology platforms, resulting in favorable incremental cost economics relative to revenue contribution.

## 投資回報分析（ROI / ROIC）

Commodities segment maintains profitability aligned with company-wide margins: gross margin 78.74%, operating margin 60.7%, and net margin 74.53%[3]. The business model generates revenue through trading fees, clearing fees, and storage/warehousing service fees. Q1 2026 data indicate the commodities market benefited from elevated trading activity, with core business income rising 22%[2], reflecting strong precious metals demand and commodity price volatility. The segment demonstrates capital-light characteristics with substantial operating leverage.

## 成長驅動因素

Commodities trading growth is driven by:
- **Precious metals demand**: Geopolitical uncertainty and portfolio diversification driving gold and silver trading
- **Commodity price volatility**: Price fluctuations in oil, metals, and agricultural products increasing hedging demand
- **Asian market growth**: Rising middle-class wealth in Asia driving investment in alternative assets
- **Inflation hedging**: Investor demand for commodity exposure as inflation protection
- **Future market expansion**: Potential introduction of new commodity products and expanded derivative offerings

## 競爭地位

The commodities segment operates in a more competitive environment than equities, with competition from:
- International commodity exchanges (London Metals Exchange, COMEX)
- Regional Asian exchanges with commodity offerings
- Over-the-counter precious metals dealers

However, 00388.HK maintains competitive advantages through:
- Regional geographic positioning for Asian commodity flows
- Integration with Hong Kong's status as a global precious metals hub
- Access to deep liquidity from regional investors
- Regulatory credibility and infrastructure quality

The segment represents strategic diversification from equities while maintaining operational synergies.
