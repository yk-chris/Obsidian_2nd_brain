### 计算光刻：用假图案刻出真芯片？

**23:12** · In a lithography machine, light will project the circuit diagram on the mask onto the wafer.

**23:17** · However, if the final circuit is directly engraved on the mask, the image may be blurry.

**23:23** · In fact, everyone learned this in high school physics class.

**23:26** · If light is allowed to pass through a very narrow slit, it will trigger the "wave" characteristics to "scatter" in all directions and enter places that were originally not illuminated . This is called diffraction . If there are two slits, the light passing through the adjacent slits will superimpose and produce interference.

**23:42** · In the end, multiple stripes of light or dark are revealed.

**23:46** · This is the famous double-slit interference experiment of light.

**23:50** · Therefore, in order to improve the accuracy of lithography, we have the aforementioned method of minimizing the wavelength of light to reduce the diffraction effect.

**23:57** · But when the wavelength of light is fixed, how can we do it?

**24:00** · This requires the use of computational lithography. Computational lithography mainly focuses on four aspects: pupil shape, light source, mask , and wavefront optimization tools.

**24:10** · Let's first look at the pupil.

**24:12** · There will be different patterns on the pupil to adjust the light into a certain shape to reduce interference in a certain direction.

**24:19** · For example, if we want to print out a shape full of vertical stripes , we use a pupil with such a pattern to reduce the interference of lateral illumination.

**24:26** · The mask circuit is ever-changing, but the pupil shape is relatively fixed, so ASML has made some optimizations to the light source module and set up an array composed of many tiny mirrors called Flex-Ray to control local illumination and thus improve the resolution of the image. At the same time, the diffraction and interference of light will make the final image distorted.

**24:47** · At this time, some additional small holes can be added to the corners of the mask pattern to increase the local light input , and the final image can be corrected.

**24:55** · This process is called optical proximity correction . Proximity Correction is referred to as OPC , but light itself also carries energy.

**25:04** · When light passes through the lens, it heats the lens. The lens expands and contracts due to heat , and the image is distorted.

**25:10** · At this time, a wavefront optimization tool is needed to calculate and heat the corresponding position of the conjugate mirror in the lens group to correct the image.

**25:17** · It is worth mentioning that computational lithography not only corrects optical errors, but also imperfections caused by other physical and chemical factors.

**25:25** · As the circuit diagram of the chip becomes more and more complex, OPC has also evolved into inverse lithography technology , referred to as ILT (Inverse Lithography Technology).

**25:33** · This is also the most important way to improve K1.
